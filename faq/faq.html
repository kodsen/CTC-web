What are the classes available for admission?

All class in all the arms is available for admission. That is, nursery 1, nursery 2, prep, primary 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5. Then J.S.S 1, 2, 3 and S.S 1, 2 and 3.

What programme(s) has been organized for staff over the years to improve service delivery?

To ensure quality of the learners, the staff frequently undergo seminars, and training in various subject matters as it affects the improvement and delivery of the teachers. In recent times, the school has partnered with the Centre for Learning and Educational Development Advocacy (CLEDA), to train staff termly and conduct career counseling for students (once a week for J.S and S.S). With this, the teachers are prepared to teach the students using current methodologies.

Is the school funded by the church?

The school's main source of income is the school fees paid by her pupils and students. At admission of learners, a bank teller is issued of the total fees payable to a designated bank. Receipt is issued by the bursar when teller copy for the school is submitted. Bank tellers are issued to all learners of the school at the end of each school term to enable parents pay their wards fees before the resumption of a new term.

Other sources of income are sales of forms, sales of the school exercise books, commission from the sales of published books, etc. All school money is payed into the school's bank account with the bank teller kept for record purposes. The school is not funded by the church.

What provision has been put in place with rergards to scholarship?

The church, the proprietor of the school gives scholarship to orphans and some fatherless members of the church who are in the academy. Beneficiaries do not pay tuition. They only pay other fees. All pastors children, irrespective of their church affiliation have a discount of 20% on tuition. Other fees are paid in full. All staff of the schools are also Beneficiaries of this largess.
