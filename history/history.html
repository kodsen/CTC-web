History of CTC Academy

Christian Teaching Center Academy otherwise known as C.T.C Academy began on the 20th of September, 1991, as a mission school. The founder of the Academy is Dr. John E. Akapmi. He is the president of the Global Revival Gospel Ministries International a.k.a Christian Teaching Center Family Church. A trained medical practionioner who resigned to pursue the calling of God in his life, in 1990.

Dr J.E Akpami has an Apostolic ministry with evidence of a ground-breaking, record-breaking, destiny-shaping and life-changing impact. His unique style of ministration makes the supernatural a common place. There is no doubt that Dr. J.E Akpami is a man sent by God for a time like this to impact his world. We are indeed proud to be part of his ministry.

Dr. J.E Akpami is happily married to his beloved wife, friend and co-minister, Pastor Clara Akpami, who is a member of the governing board of C.T.C Academy. Together, they are blessed with two children: Eunice and Paul. Dr. J.E Akpami and his wife live in Zaria, Nigeria, from where they are fulfilling their life's purposes.

THE MANDATE

Christian Teaching Center Academy was inaugurated on the need to attain academic excellence without compromising the spiritual lives of the learners. Upon this foundation was the school established.

In 2003, the president related an article "The birth of C.T.C Academy" published by the Academy; how that a J.S.S student returned from school one day with some occultic books freely distributed to them by one of their teachers. He explained further, how that one of the books taught innocent students how to build simple alters (shrines) by their bedsides.

The birth of C.T.C Academy was to prevent students from earning distinctions and credits at the expense of their souls. C.T.C Academy was birthed to ensure that students do not end up being initiated into the occults and demonic cult groups in pursuit of education.

To avoid exposing learners to such risk C.T.C Academy has a mandate to provide the best education possible under strong spiritual covering. This as a matter of urgency, informed the vision of C.T.C Academy.

THE INCEPTION OF THE ACADEMY

At the inception of the academy in September 20th 1999, the school had only the the nursery and primary sections, headed by Mr. Ezra Cirang. As Head master, Mr. Ezra was appointed to oversee and grow the 32 pupils and few staff, among which were: Mrs. Manji Abu (now Barrister Manji Abu), Miss. Esther O.A., Miss. Comfort Oguda (now late) and Mr. Mathew Sule (who was awaiting service in NYSC and offered to assist as a teacher).

One year later in September 2000, the secondary section of the school took off with 15 J.S.S 1 students. Following this development, Mr. Ezra was then appointed Principal of the Academy, to oversee the various sections of the school. To help the newly appointed Principal take the school to a greater height, few more teachers were employed.

As a means to foster effective management of the school, the President, Dr. J.E Akpami constituted a Governing Board of five membership, comprising of Mr S.A Alagbe as Chairman; Pastor(Mrs.) Clara Akpami. Pastor Adogbo, Brother Dapo and Mr. Ezra Cirang as the Secretary of the Board. This Board was tasked with the responsibility of admission regulation, employment of staff and also undertake major decisions that affects the school.

At this point, basic and essential facilities were begining to be put in place for proper and smooth running of the school. Mr. Ezra had hardly began the registration of the school with the appropriate educational authorities when resigned his appointment with the school in December 2002, to pursue his political ambition.

It was at this point that Mrs. Edna Onyenobi- A Masters Degree in English Language holder, was appointed Principal, to take up the task of taking CTC Academy to its promised land. In December 2002, when Mrs. Edna took over the leadership of the Academy as Principal, the school had about 55 students already registered in the secondary school and 77 students in the nursery and primary school. With about 15 teachers, she was to steer the Academy to a greater height.

Notably among her first achievement as the Principal of the school was the registration of the school with the Kaduna State Education Authority, which also approved a J.S.S Examination Center for the school. This was very timely because the school at this point had progressed to having J.S.S 3 students, ready to write Junior WAEC. In 2004, following the graduation of students from J.S.S 3 to S.S 1, it became necessary for additional classrooms to be provided. This was what informed the building in October. This building produced three classes with a staffroom. In 2005, the building of the examination hall and the computer room began and was completed the same year. With the building of the hall came additional classrooms.

In October 2008, the Academy got the approval for the conduction of NECO and WAEC Examinations in the school premises. By this time, the school was accredited as NECO and WAEC centres. Following this development, update of facilities of the Academy became necessary. The scientific lab was upgraded to this regard. In 2009, a set of students were set to write their WAEC Examination. Following the progress the school was making, in 2009, the Parents Teachers Association (P.T.A) body which was inaugurated at the inception of the school, suddenly jerked to life with a move to build a library for the school.

The body under the Chairmanship of Mr. Tunde Pratt donated the sum of N400,000 for the building of the library with a promise to complete it. However, the Academy under the leadership of Mrs. Edna Onyenobi-The Principal- went further to complete the building using the school fund. The The building was completed in 2010 and had since been put to use as Library.
